Part II - Kitty of Angels Adoption vs. Donation Campaigns¶

by Matthew Morrow¶

Investigation Overview¶

Introduction¶

For context, I am the Google Ads Manager for Kitty of Angels, a 501(c)(3) non-profit devoted to supporting homeless cats and kittens in Los Angeles. The primary goal of this analysis was to assess the performance and impact of our Google Ads Donation campaign in comparison to the Google Ads Adoption campaign.

In June, preliminary observations hinted that the Google Ads Donation campaign might not have a strong correlation with an uptick in donations. This assessment was initially based on a cursory review of charts on the Google Ads platform. The apparent return on investment did not seem to justify the budget allocation, especially when contrasted against the adoption campaign's results. Consequently, I opted to temporarily pause the Donation campaign, redirecting funds to the Adoption campaign instead, awaiting a deeper, data-driven examination.

Key Findings¶

  • The exploratory analysis emphasized that click engagement is variable, with no discernible consistent trend. This variability suggests unpredictable fluctuations in user engagement over time.

  • There's noticeable disparity in costs associated with daily clicks. Some days, even with considerable advertising expenditure, the engagement (clicks) was not proportionally high, hinting that higher spending doesn't always guarantee increased engagement.

  • In the realm of adoptions, specific primary breeds, like the Domestic Shorthair, exhibited distinct patterns, both in terms of engagement and the related costs. This observation suggests that breed popularity might play a role in engagement levels.

Insights¶

The data narrative offers insights that challenge and refine my initial assumptions. While I initially believed the Donation campaign might be underperforming, the data paints a more nuanced picture. There were instances where significant advertising spend did not result in a corresponding increase in engagement. Such patterns could be influenced by myriad factors, including ad content quality, the Google Ads platform, or even external events that could be swaying user behavior.

In contrast, the Adoption campaign highlighted intriguing patterns. Notably, certain breeds, especially the Domestic Shorthair, manifested higher engagement levels. Such insights pave the way for more granular inquiries, perhaps probing whether specific breeds inherently draw more attention and, subsequently, result in increased adoptions.

Visualization 1: Combined Histogram for 'Clicks' Distribution¶

Purpose: To compare the distribution of 'Clicks' for both the Adoption and Donation campaigns.

In [ ]:
# Plotting a histogram with a density line overlay
plt.figure(figsize=(14,6))
sns.histplot(ads_merged, x='Clicks', hue='Campaign Type', element='step', stat='density', common_norm=False, bins=30)

# Applying a KDE line overlay
sns.kdeplot(data=ads_merged, x='Clicks', hue='Campaign Type', fill=True, alpha=0.2)

plt.title('Distribution of Clicks for Adoption vs. Donation Campaigns', fontsize=16, fontweight='bold')
plt.xlabel('Number of Clicks', fontsize=12)
plt.ylabel('Density', fontsize=12)
plt.legend(title='Campaign Type', loc='upper right', labels=['Adoption', 'Donation'])
plt.grid(axis='both', linestyle='--', alpha=0.7)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Comments on "Distribution of 'Clicks' for Adoption vs. Donation Campaigns":¶

  • This histogram provides a clear comparison of the clicks distribution between the Adoption and Donation campaigns.
  • Observations from the histogram:

    • The Adoption campaign displays a broader distribution with a wider range of clicks, suggesting a diverse engagement pattern.
    • The Donation campaign, on the other hand, has its density clustered around a smaller range of clicks, indicating a more uniform but potentially lower engagement level.

  • The overlaying KDE (Kernel Density Estimation) plot provides a smooth representation of the data's distribution, further emphasizing the differences in engagement between the two campaigns.
  • The density normalization of the histogram ensures an unbiased comparison between the two campaigns by taking into account the variations in their individual data counts.
  • In conclusion, the visualization reinforces the idea that the Adoption campaign tends to have higher and varied engagement, while the Donation campaign demonstrates a more consistent but possibly lower engagement pattern.

plt.figure(figsize=(14,6))

# Setting the background style
sns.set_style("whitegrid")

sns.boxplot(data=ads_merged, x='Campaign Type', y='Cost', palette="Set2",)

# Overlaying swarmplot with jitter and adjusted alpha
sns.swarmplot(data=ads_merged, x='Campaign Type', y='Cost', color=".25", alpha=0.7, size=5)

# Annotating with median values
median_values = {'Adoption': 107.03, 'Donation': 68.10} # Rounded to 2 decimal places
for i, (campaign_type, median) in enumerate(median_values.items()):
    x_position = i + 0.2
    y_offset = 3 if campaign_type == 'Adoption' else -3
    plt.text(x_position, median + y_offset, f"Median: ${median:.2f}", horizontalalignment='center', verticalalignment='center', fontweight='bold', color='black')

plt.title('Distribution of Advertising Spend by Campaign Type', fontsize=16, fontweight='bold', pad=20)
plt.xlabel('Campaign Type', fontsize=12, labelpad=10)
plt.ylabel('Cost ($)', fontsize=12, labelpad=10)
plt.tight_layout()
plt.show()

Comments on "Distribution of Advertising Spend by 'Campaign Type'":¶

  • The boxplots offer a clear and detailed overview of the advertising expenditure for both the Adoption and Donation campaigns.
  • Key Observations:

    • Both campaigns exhibit varied spending patterns, but the Donation campaign appears to have a higher median spend compared to the Adoption campaign.
    • The Adoption campaign's boxplot suggests a more concentrated range of expenditure, with fewer outliers.
    • On the other hand, the Donation campaign shows a wider interquartile range, hinting at more variability in daily ad spends.
    • Notably, the presence of potential outliers in the Donation campaign might indicate days with exceptionally high ad spends.

  • The visualization prompts deeper inquiry: Does the higher median expenditure in the Donation campaign translate to better engagement? Or does the Adoption campaign achieve better results with a lower budget?
